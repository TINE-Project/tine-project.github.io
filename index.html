Translation is Not Enough
Plain Language Adaptation
of Multilingual Science

The TINE project produces novel accessible machine translation technology for the production of science in your own language. Across 4 countries and 5 universities, we support 4 use cases putting plain-language, own-language science into the hands of those who need it.

Project Summary

Open Science mandates exist across Europe indicating that science must be made accessible beyond the borders of academia affecting publishers and scientists alike.

However, simply sharing science as publicly available does not make it accessible. Scientists are constrained to write in the languages accepted by major publication venues and the languages of reviewers. Science communication written in a mother tongue is accessible only to a home audience, limiting international impact. Science communication requires accessibility to build impact. If the output of translation is complex technical language we only overcome one accessibility barrier, but many more remain.

Translation is not enough. We need simple, clear and accessible cross-lingual science communication. The goal of the TINE project is to enable both producers and lay readers of science communication to access clear scientific information in their own language, promoting multilingualism.

Use Cases

The TINE project is structured around four Use Cases, which will drive technological requirements for the development of accessible scientific machine translation technology.

🇪🇸

Use Case 1: Accessing Pan-European Grant Databases

This task, in collaboration with SIRIS Academic, will implement a solution to explore international research databases, translating project descriptions and evaluating the usefulness of concept explanations for expert readers.

🇵🇱

Use Case 2: Translating Scientific Papers in Polish for Peer Review

This task will enable wider access to Polish-language papers, increasing reviewer diversity in a scientific journal and improving both the quality of peer review and the visibility of Polish science.

🏴󠁧󠁢󠁷󠁬󠁳󠁿

Use Case 3: Improving Accessibility for Welsh-Speaking Communities in Social Care Research

This task creates accurate, culturally relevant Welsh-language translations of research materials for social care workers, ensuring clarity, accessibility, and alignment with regional dialects for Welsh-speaking service users.

🇨🇳

Use Case 4: Supporting Chinese Students in UK Scientific Degrees

This task embeds accessible English-to-Chinese translation of science-specific academic materials at MMJI, enhancing student understanding and evaluating the impact on academic outcomes in collaboration with Hubei University.
