About the Group

A multidisciplinary team pushing the boundaries of how machines understand and generate human language.

The MMU NLP Group is part of the Department of Computing and Mathematics at Manchester Metropolitan University. We conduct cutting-edge research in natural language processing and computational linguistics, with a strong emphasis on real-world impact across healthcare, finance, social media, and education.

Our group brings together academics, researchers, and PhD students who share a passion for language technology. We are committed to open science, responsible AI, and inclusive research that benefits society.

Research Themes

From foundational models to applied systems, our work spans the full spectrum of language AI.

🧠

Large Language Models

Pre-training, fine-tuning, and evaluation of large-scale language models. Investigating in-context learning, instruction-following, and emergent capabilities.

💬

Dialogue & Conversational AI

Building goal-oriented and open-domain dialogue systems. Research in multi-turn reasoning, dialogue state tracking, and response generation.

🔎

Information Extraction

Extracting structured knowledge from unstructured text. Named entity recognition, relation extraction, event detection, and knowledge graph construction.

🌐

Multilingual NLP

Cross-lingual transfer, low-resource language processing, and machine translation. Building robust NLP systems for under-resourced languages.

🩺

Biomedical & Clinical NLP

Text mining from electronic health records, clinical trial analysis, biomedical literature understanding, and disease surveillance.

😊

Sentiment & Opinion Analysis

Aspect-level sentiment analysis, opinion mining from social media, emotion detection, and stance classification at scale.

🔍

Question Answering & Retrieval

Open-domain and domain-specific QA systems, dense retrieval augmented generation, reading comprehension, and fact verification.

🛡️

Responsible & Ethical AI

Bias detection and mitigation in NLP models, fairness, explainability, and misinformation detection for trustworthy language systems.
