Translation is Not Enough (TINE): Plain Language Adaptation of Multilingual Science

TINE is a project focused on turning multilingual scientific writing into clear, plain-language summaries that preserve the core meaning, scientific nuance, and multilingual context.

Back to Projects See Research Themes

Overview

Translation is not always enough for scientific communication. TINE explores how multilingual scientific documents can be translated and adapted for wider audiences, while keeping technical accuracy, meaning, and scholarly context intact.

The project combines whole-document translation, domain adaptation, terminology decoding, and user-focused dissemination so science can be consumed without unnecessary barriers.

Consortium

  • Pompeu Fabra University (Barcelona)
  • Cardiff University
  • University of Zurich
  • Institute of Computer Sciences, Polish Academy of Science
  • MMU NLP Lab

Funding and duration

  • Funded through the CHIST-ERA network
  • Duration: 36 months
  • Start date: May 2026

Initial focus

  • Objective O.1 - Develop state-of-the-art methods for whole-document translation and domain adaptation
  • Objective O.2 - Democratisation of access to scholarly documents to enable end-users to consume science without barriers
  • Objective O.3 - Decode scientific terminologies and style to fit the needs of lay science consumers
  • Objective O.4 - Demonstrate to user communities the practical use of the developed technology
